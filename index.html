About Me

From Latin America
to machine learning

I'm a UC Berkeley MIDS student with a background in international development, where I spent several years working on efforts to end violence against women and children across Latin America. It was there that I saw firsthand how data — collected carefully, analyzed rigorously, and communicated clearly — could move people and drive real decisions.

That realization sent me down a path I haven't looked back from: building models, designing tools, and extracting insights that don't just describe the world but help change it. I enjoy figuring out how complex systems work and finding ways to improve processes that generate broad impact and inform decision making.
